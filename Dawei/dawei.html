Dawei

Hidden Heaven
Of The Local

Dawei is a city in south-eastern Myanmar and is the capital of the Tanintharyi Region, formerly known as the Tenasserim Division, about 614.3 km south of Yangon on the northern bank of the Dawei River. Population is 139,900. Dawei is a port at the head of the Dawei River estuary, 30 km. from the Andaman Sea.

Muang Ma Kan beach

Muang Ma Kan beach is located 19 kilometres from the city, in between hills on one side and the sea on the other. A half an hour walk down the beach takes visitors to a lovely fishing village with small boats nestled in its harbour.

Myaw Yit Pagoda

Myaw Yit Pagoda is a Buddhist pagoda located in Dawei off the coast of Maungmagan Beach. It is situated on a small island surrounded by the sea, and that makes it worth visiting as the temple isn't otherwise unique. A wooden bridge connects the pagoda with the shore, and there are a few viewpoints near the temple which are hotspots among the tourists for witnessing gorgeous sunsets.

Shwe Taung Sar

Shwe Taung Sar Pagoda is located in the centre of Dawei. It’s one of the famous  pagodas in Dawei. Within the pagoda area, there are 28 Buddha Statues, Law Ka Marazein Statue and a museum. Many festivals are held at the Shwe Taung Sar Pagoda. Thadingyut festival and Tazaungdaing festival are the most celebrated festivals.

Shwe Thayaung Pagoda

Shwe Thalyaung Pagoda is located in Kanine Dar Village, Dawei Township, Dawei District, Tanintharyi. It is an image of the second biggest reclining buddha in Myanmar. Every year, local people celebrate the festival at the ShweThalyaung pagoda in February.

Shin Koe Shin

Shin Koe Shin Pagodas are nine unique pagodas in Dawei. So, make sure you visit at least one of these pagodas to get a sense of local religious traditions. The Shin Koe Shin Pagodas are Shin Hsandaw Shin Pagoda, Shin Dat Wel Pagoda, Shin Zalun Pagoda, Shin Okaw  Pagoda, Shin Pin Khayu Pagoda, Shin Tauk Htein Pagoda, Shin Mokhtee Pagoda, Shin Taunt Po Pagoda and Shin Maw Pagoda. These nine pagodas are different pagodas in different areas but their histories of pagodas are connected.

Dawei has many waterfalls and the best time to visit them is in the rainy season which is from May to October. These waterfalls are Taw Gel waterfall, Sin Koe Yap waterfall, Angel waterfall, Tizit waterfall, Thayetchaung waterfall, Thin Gan Tone waterfall and Kalonehtar waterfall.

Grandfather Beach is located in Nyaung Pyin village, Launglone township, Dawei district, Tanintharyi. It is 71 km away from Dawei and takes 2 hours by car or motorbike. It is a beautiful beach with crystal clear water and soft white sand. It is the best place to relax, take photos, swim and watch the sunset view at the beach.
