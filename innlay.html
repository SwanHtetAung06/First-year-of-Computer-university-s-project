Gripping Information

Inlay Lake, the second-largest lake in Myanmar, is located in the heart of the country. Surrounded by mountains, it offers a breathtaking view of nature. There are 17 villages on the shore and islands on Inle Lake. Each village has its own culture and traditions which are unique in its own way.

Inlay Lake offers one of its kind shopping experience. Each village has its own floating market where traders wander and sell their products.

A trip here gives you the chance to connect with the locals and learn about their traditional methods of farming, fishing, cooking, and crafting. The villagers are warm and friendly, eager to share their way of life with visitors. You’ll feel their genuine hospitality when you interact with them. Explore Inle Lake and experience nature in a way you’ve never seen before

People live in houses made on stilts Spending a night or two at stilt houses is one of the main attractions of Inle Lake.The experience is so captivating that tourists are eager to spend a night or two there.

Inlay Floating Market

Inle Lake’s floating market is a must-visit destination. This daily market meets the common shopping needs of locals and rotates around five towns in the Shan State: Nyaung Shwe, Heho, Taunggyi, Minethauk, and Shwe Nyaung. Each town hosts the market every fifth day, providing a vibrant trading hub for local tribes to sell their fresh farm produce.

Why Visit?

The Five-Day Market is a fantastic way to experience the culture of Inle Lake. It provides visitors with an excellent opportunity to meet friendly locals from different tribes and witness their warm generosity.

Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda

Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda is one of the holiest places in Shan State. The shrine is massive and houses five ancient golden Buddha statues. There’s also this huge golden barge, a replica of the one King Alaung Sithu supposedly used for his travels. During the annual pagoda festival, this barge gets towed by long boats, visiting over 20 villagers.

Back in 1965, when the barge was carrying the five Buddha images, it sank, and all the statues fell into the lake. People dived in to search and managed to recover four of them, but the fifth one was missing. After a long search, they gave up and returned to the pagoda — only to find the missing image miraculously back in its shrine. Since then, the fifth Buddha image always stays in its shrine during the boat tours.

The pagoda festival runs for 18 days between September and October during Thadingyut. It’s a must-visit for both locals and tourists, especially because of the iconic rowing competitions between villages, where rowers use the unique one-leg rowing technique.

History and Background

Shwe Inn Dein Pagoda is the groups of stupas in the Inn Dein Village of Inle lake in Shan State, Myanmar. The pagodas still remains its architectures and structure back to ancient times. Traditions holds that they were built by King Ashoka and renewed by King Anawrahta. There are around 1000 pagodas in this area.

Why should visit?

A pagoda which you should definitely visit when you are at Inle Lake is Shwe Indein Pagoda. There is a Buddha image placed in a white-washed stupa in this pagoda. Below the stupa, you will find hundreds of these stupas close to hundreds of years old. This pagoda is in Idein Village and it takes a 5 kilometres boat ride to reach this place from Ywama Village. The boat ride itself will take you through Inle Lake and enable you to explore the best of the lake.

When

The best time to visit is in the morning when you can explore the histories of pagodas and take beautiful photos. The opening hours is within 8:00 am to 6:30 pm.

HOW TO GET THERE

• Inn Dein village is located about 8 kilometers from the Inle Lake. The easiest way to get there is by boat which you can experience the surrounding nature and fresh air. You can easily booked at your hotel for the boat trip to Shwe Inn Dein Pagoda.
